Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dance. To answer you question, my favourite dances are as follows, with defnitions:

Polynesian chicken dance
A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock or be "fruitful".
Icelandic brownian shuffle
Before the Icelanders developted fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which involved hudding close together in a circle oon the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptably tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired modern styles such as Twerking.
Arctic robot dance
An interresting examole of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northem Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they are just moving like this because they were really cold.
